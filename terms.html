02

What Bingwa Flash Provides

Bingwa Flash is an Android automation and business tool for Bingwa Sokoni agents. It helps agents detect M-Pesa payment SMS messages, match payments to configured offers, run USSD steps, manage clients, send configured responses, publish web order pages, and use relay or portal features where enabled.

Bingwa Flash is not Safaricom, M-Pesa, a bank, or a mobile network operator. Safaricom and M-Pesa services remain controlled by their respective providers.

03

Accounts and Security

Agents must provide accurate account details and keep their login credentials secure. One account should be used only by its authorised owner or business. We may use device-session controls to protect accounts from unauthorised simultaneous access.

Clients who register on the website may be required to set a PIN to protect their registration. Users must not share security PINs, app credentials, M-Pesa PINs, or device unlock codes.

04

Agent Responsibilities

Agents are responsible for their offers, prices, USSD codes, SIM selection, relay rules, verifier settings, response templates, contact-saving settings, and web order settings.

  • Verify every offer before enabling automation.
  • Keep enough airtime, tokens, network access, permissions, and device battery for automation to run.
  • Monitor failed, pending, scheduled, executing, and not-verified transactions.
  • Resolve customer disputes, refunds, wrong numbers, duplicate requests, and failed fulfilment promptly and honestly.
  • Comply with Safaricom, M-Pesa, tax, consumer-protection, and communications laws that apply to your business.
  • Ensure your published order page shows accurate offers and pricing at all times.
05

Automation and USSD

The app may use SMS, accessibility, phone, contacts, notification, overlay, and background-service permissions to automate transactions. Automation can fail because of wrong mappings, changed Safaricom menus, network issues, insufficient airtime, missing permissions, app shutdown, SIM problems, duplicate sessions, device restrictions, or third-party changes.

Agents must test mappings and review transaction logs. Bingwa Flash helps automate work, but the agent remains responsible for confirming that customers receive the correct product or service.

06

Web Orders

Agents may publish a public order page using a username. The page may show public business details, enabled offers, pricing, order availability, and verification status. Agents must ensure their public information and offers are accurate and up to date.

Customers using web orders must provide accurate payment and receiving phone numbers. Orders may be delayed, rejected, or require manual follow-up if payment details are invalid, an offer is unavailable, or the agent device cannot complete automation.

07

Payment Intermediary Role

When a customer pays through an agent's web order page, Bingwa Flash acts solely as a payment intermediary. The M-Pesa STK push is sent on behalf of the agent's configured till or paybill, and funds collected are forwarded to the agent almost immediately after Safaricom confirms the payment.

Bingwa Flash does not hold customer funds beyond the time needed to process the transaction. Once funds are forwarded to the agent, all matters relating to the product, service, delivery, and any refund are strictly between the customer and the agent. Bingwa Flash will not be liable for the agent's failure to deliver.

Bingwa Flash may attempt to assist in resolving a dispute only if funds are clearly documented as still held at the time the complaint is raised and the issue is reported promptly to support@bingwaflash.co.ke.

08

Clients and Contacts

Client lists are primarily controlled by the agent. Agents may import clients from messages or files, save them locally, and optionally save them to the selected device contact storage. Website client registration is optional and disabled unless the agent enables it.

Clients may request removal from an agent list where the website removal flow is available, or by contacting the agent directly.

09

Relay and Portal

Relay features are intended for agreed agent-to-agent fulfilment workflows. Agents must enable only the relay rules they trust. Portal features allow authorised website sessions to view or control selected app functions only when enabled by the agent.

Agents must not use relay or portal tools to impersonate another agent, intercept messages without consent, or run transactions they are not authorised to perform.

Verification Badge

The verification badge is tied to the agent name, business name, agent phone number, and till number. If those details change, verification may be disabled until the new details are reviewed. A badge is not a guarantee of transaction success, refund eligibility, or endorsement by Safaricom.
